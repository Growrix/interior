# Product Requirements

## Product Summary

The Interior Design Platform is a responsive, image-forward web platform that helps interior designers present their portfolios and helps potential clients discover designers, evaluate fit, start conversations, and book consultations. Supporting engagement surfaces include blog content, events/workshops, e-commerce, and an AI assistant.

## Problem Statement

Interior designers need a credible digital surface that converts visual proof of work into qualified interest. Potential clients need a trusted way to discover designers by location, style, budget, and project type without excessive friction.

## Goals

### Primary goals
- Make designer discovery visually compelling and operationally clear.
- Convert browsing behavior into inquiries and consultation bookings.
- Establish trust through reviews, portfolio depth, and consistent profile data.
- Support repeat engagement through content, events, and commerce.

### Experience goals
- Preserve a premium, calm, image-led aesthetic.
- Keep navigation simple across desktop, tablet, and mobile.
- Deliver an app-like mobile web experience focused on touch friendliness and clarity.

## Confirmed In Scope

- Multi-designer public discovery experience
- Designer profiles and portfolios
- Search and filtering by key user-facing dimensions
- Client inquiry, consultation booking, and messaging capability
- Reviews and ratings
- Blog and events/workshops surfaces
- Product commerce capability
- AI assistant for navigation, recommendations, and general guidance

## Confirmed Out Of Scope For This Phase

- Native mobile app planning
- Advanced 3D/AR planning tools
- Enterprise procurement or SSO requirements
- Full social-network behavior beyond the requested communication flows
- Deep technical implementation commitments not yet approved by specialized phases

## Planning-Level Functional Outcomes

- A visitor can discover designers through browse, search, filter, and content entry paths.
- A visitor can evaluate a designer through profile, portfolio, budget/style indicators, and reviews.
- A client can initiate contact and move toward consultation booking.
- A designer can maintain a profile, portfolio, and reputation-bearing presence.
- An operator can govern trust-sensitive areas such as reviews, profiles, and public content.

## Experience Directives

- High-quality images are a primary content type and shape the information architecture.
- Public surfaces must remain simple, elegant, and browsing-friendly.
- Mobile UX must remain app-like in feel, but exact navigation components are deferred to frontend planning.
- Later UX planning may use icon-first compact patterns on mobile only if clarity and accessibility remain strong.

## Constraints

- The platform must remain stack-agnostic at this stage.
- The shared contract must support later specialization without locking unapproved implementation details.
- Trust-sensitive features such as reviews, messaging, booking, commerce, and AI require later hardening.
- Media-heavy experiences require strong performance discipline.

## Assumptions

- The platform is marketplace-oriented rather than a single-studio brochure site.
- Designer and client accounts both exist.
- Booking is consultation-oriented, not full project delivery management.
- Commerce can be platform-run, designer-run, or affiliate-assisted; exact model is unresolved.

## Open Questions

- What is the primary revenue model: subscription, lead fee, transaction fee, ads, or hybrid?
- Which regions, languages, and currencies must be supported first?
- Is designer verification mandatory before public listing?
- Are client accounts required before initiating contact or booking?
- Are events informational listings only, or do they require registration and attendance workflows?
